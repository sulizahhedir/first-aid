First Aid:Choking Emergency

Concious Adult Choking

When someone is choking, they may be unable to breathe properly. Find out what to look for and what to do.

What to do:








Click on the video to learn more:

1. Ask the casualty, “Are you choking?”

2. Encourage the casualty to cough

3. If they cannot clear the object themselves, support them with one hand while leaning them forwards:

  • Give up to 5 back blows between shoulder blades.

  • Check their mouth

4. If unsuccessful, try up to 5 abdominal thrusts:

  • Stand behind the casualty and link your hands below their rib cage with the lower hand clenched in a fist. Pull sharply inwards and upwards.

  • Check their mouth

5. If the casualty is still choking dial 999 for the emergency services.

6. Repeat step 3 & 4, checking the mouth after each step until help arrives.

7. If the casualty becomes unresponsive at any point during the procedure, dial 999 for the emergency services immediately and start resuscitation.

8. If abdominal thrust are used, seek medical services.

Concious Infant Choking

As you start weaning your baby, they may be at risk of choking. Find out what to look for and what to do.

What to do:








Click on the video to learn more:

1. Never use abdominal thrust on an infant.

2. Lay the infant face down on your thigh while supporting their head. Give the infant up to five back blows.

3. Turn the infant over so they are lying on your other thigh and check their mouth for obvious obstructions (do not do a blind finger sweep). If choking persist, try up to five chest thrusts.

4. Place two fingers on the breastbone, one fingers breadth below the nipple line and push downwards.

5. Check the mouth.

6. If the obstruction has not cleared, dial 999 for emergency help using your mobile on speaker phone. Taking the infant with you is necessary.

7. Continue the cycles of back blows and chest thrust while waiting for the emergency services to arrive.

8. If the infant becomes unresponsive, carry out resuscitation for one minute. Then dial 999 for an ambulance taking the infant with you.
